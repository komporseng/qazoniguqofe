Jenny Han Professional Life

During her college years, Jenny authored her first book, the children’s novel Shug, which was released in 2006. She later worked on a young adult romance trilogy during her summer breaks.

From 2009 to 2011, she worked on three novels released by Simon & Schuster: The Summer I Turned Pretty, It’s Not Summer Without You, and We’ll Always Have Summer, which became New York Times Best Sellers.

She also co-wrote a young adult trilogy with Siobhan Vivian, beginning with the 2012 release Burn for Burn, which included paranormal and romance elements.

In 2014, she published To All the Boys I’ve Loved Before, a young adult romantic novel which quickly garnered attention for a film adaptation. The movie, starring Lana Condor, was released by Netflix in August 2018 and was well-received.

She also made a brief appearance in the film, and sequels titled P.S. I Still Love You and To All the Boys: Always and Forever were released in 2020 and 2021 respectively.

Recently, a TV series based on her novel series The Summer I Turned Pretty was launched on Amazon Prime in June 2022. Jenny will be involved in the upcoming romantic dramedy streaming series, XO, Kitty, serving as a writer, executive producer, and showrunner. The story will follow Lara’s sister, Kitty Covey, in her quest for true love.
