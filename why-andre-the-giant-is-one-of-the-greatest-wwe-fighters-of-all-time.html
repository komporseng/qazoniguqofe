Why Andre The Giant is one of the greatest WWE Fighters of All-Time

August 2024 Â· 3 minute read

Andre the Giant, also known as "The Eighth Wonder of the World," was a larger-than-life figure in the world of professional wrestling. Standing at 7 feet 4 inches tall and weighing over 500 pounds, Andre's impact on the WWE (then known as WWF) and the wrestling industry as a whole is undeniable. Here are several reasons why Andre the Giant is considered one of the greatest WWE fighters of all time.

Unmatched Physical Presence

One of the most obvious reasons for Andre's legendary status in the WWE is his unparalleled physical presence. His extraordinary height and immense weight made him a spectacle to behold in the ring. This larger-than-life stature not only made him a formidable opponent but also added an element of spectacle and excitement to every match he was a part of.

Unbeatable Streak

Throughout his career, Andre the Giant maintained an almost unbeatable streak in the wrestling world. His dominance in the ring and his ability to overpower opponents, often much smaller than him, solidified his reputation as a force to be reckoned with. This unbeatable streak contributed significantly to his status as one of the greatest WWE fighters of all time.

Iconic Matches and Rivalries

Andre the Giant was involved in several iconic matches and rivalries that have become the stuff of legend in the WWE. Perhaps his most famous match was against Hulk Hogan at WrestleMania III in 1987, where he suffered his first televised loss. This match, and the build-up to it, captivated audiences and showcased Andre's ability to draw in fans and create unforgettable moments in WWE history.

Cultural Impact

Beyond his in-ring accomplishments, Andre the Giant had a significant cultural impact. His appearances in movies and television shows, such as "The Princess Bride," helped elevate his status to that of a pop culture icon. This crossover appeal brought mainstream attention to the WWE and helped solidify Andre's legacy as one of the most recognizable figures in the world of professional wrestling.

Enduring Legacy

Even after his passing in 1993, Andre the Giant's legacy continues to loom large over the WWE. His impact on the wrestling industry is still felt today, with his name and image being synonymous with the grandeur and spectacle of professional wrestling. The annual "Andre the Giant Memorial Battle Royal" at WrestleMania serves as a testament to his enduring legacy and the lasting impression he made on the WWE universe.

Personal Charisma

Despite his imposing physical presence, Andre the Giant was known for his affable and charismatic personality outside the ring. His larger-than-life persona extended beyond his size, endearing him to fans and fellow wrestlers alike. His ability to connect with audiences and fellow wrestlers added another dimension to his greatness and solidified his status as a beloved figure in the WWE community.

Andre the Giant's impact on the WWE and the wrestling world at large is immeasurable. His unmatched physical presence, unbeatable streak, iconic matches and rivalries, cultural impact, enduring legacy, and personal charisma all contribute to his status as one of the greatest WWE fighters of all time. Andre the Giant will forever be remembered as a true legend in the world of professional wrestling, leaving behind a legacy that continues to inspire and awe fans around the globe.
